The weekly review problem

The status truth lives in too many places.

SolarAPP+ Symbium AHJ portal Email/PDF CRM Spreadsheet External owner
Aged rows drift

Permit managers need stale jobs visible before they affect install readiness.

Revision loops lose shape

Category, received date, resubmitted date, and repeat-AHJ patterns often disappear from the review rhythm.

External-owner work arrives late

Vendor-owned rows can sit outside the team queue until the schedule is already exposed.

Generic stages miss the permit job

CRM fields rarely enforce owner, blocker reason, due date, and next action at permit-review granularity.

V1 operating record

One queue after design approval. Deliberately narrow.

QueuePermit tracks

  • AHJ, submission mode, owner, status, age, blocker, due date, next action.
  • Revision category, received date, resubmitted date, repeat-AHJ flag.
  • AHJ notes approved by the permit manager.
  • Weekly KPIs: median ready-to-approved, revision rate, aged jobs, unknown owner/blocker rate.

QueuePermit does not

  • Submit permits or call AHJs for you.
  • Generate plan sets, automate PTO, or dispatch inspections.
  • Replace SolarAPP+, Symbium, CRMs, or design tools.
  • Claim faster approvals, ROI, paid pilots, or validated demand.

90-day proof sprint

Run the pilot around your real weekly permit review.

  1. 01Import

    Choose 8-10 recurring launch-corridor AHJs.

  3. 03Normalize

    Assign status, owner, blocker, due date, revision state, and next action.

  4. 04Review

FAQ

Questions permit managers should ask before a pilot.

Does QueuePermit submit permits?

No. QueuePermit tracks post-design permit operations across the paths your team already uses.

Is this a replacement for SolarAPP+ or Symbium?

No. If a job qualifies for an instant permit path, use it. QueuePermit helps the permit team see the whole mixed queue.

Is this a CRM?

No. QueuePermit is narrower than a CRM and more permit-specific than a generic board.

What outcomes can we expect?

The pilot measures queue quality and operating cadence first. QueuePermit does not promise guaranteed approval speed.
